Time Blocking

‚òÖ ‚òÖ ‚òÖ

Time Blocking is a productivity method where you schedule every part of your day. Instead of a to-do list, you assign every task a specific time "block" in your calendar. It's about being intentional with your time.

üí° What It Is

Popularized by authors like Cal Newport, time blocking is a proactive approach to managing your day. You divide your day into blocks and dedicate each block to a specific task or group of tasks.

For example, instead of just "study for exam," your calendar would say:
‚Ä¢ 9:00 AM - 9:45 AM: Review Chemistry Chapter 4
‚Ä¢ 9:45 AM - 10:00 AM: Break
‚Ä¢ 10:00 AM - 11:00 AM: Do Math Problem Set

üß† Why It Works

It eliminates "decision fatigue." You don't waste mental energy deciding what to do next; you just look at your calendar and start. This lowers the barrier to getting started on difficult tasks (procrastination).

It also provides a realistic view of your time. A to-do list is infinite, but a day only has 24 hours. Blocking forces you to prioritize and allocate your time, ensuring that important tasks (like studying) don't get pushed aside by urgent ones.

‚öôÔ∏è How To Use It

1

List Your Tasks

At the start of the day (or the night before), list everything you need to do. Include classes, studying, meals, and breaks.

2

Estimate Time

Be realistic about how long each task will take. It's better to overestimate than underestimate.

3

Block Your Calendar

Open a digital calendar (or a paper planner) and create blocks for every single task, including breaks and travel time.

4

Include Buffer Time

Add 15-30 minute "buffer" blocks for unexpected interruptions or tasks that run long. This keeps one delay from ruining your whole day.

5

Follow & Adjust

Try to stick to the schedule. If you get off track, don't give up! Just adjust the rest of your day's blocks and carry on.
