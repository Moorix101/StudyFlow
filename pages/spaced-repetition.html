Spaced Repetition

‚òÖ ‚òÖ ‚òÖ

Spaced Repetition is a powerful memory technique that involves reviewing information at increasing intervals over time. Instead of cramming, you strategically review material just as you're about to forget it.

üí° What It Is

This method is based on the "forgetting curve," a concept developed by psychologist Hermann Ebbinghaus. He found that we forget information exponentially unless we review it.

Spaced Repetition directly counters this curve. Each time you successfully recall a piece of information, you increase the time until the next review (e.g., 1 day, 3 days, 1 week, 2 weeks, 1 month). This process moves information from your short-term to your long-term memory.

üß† Why It Works

It works by forcing your brain to engage in active recall at the most optimal moment‚Äîright before you forget. This retrieval effort signals to your brain that the information is important, strengthening the neural pathway.

Reviewing information you already know well is inefficient. Reviewing it too late means you have to re-learn it. Spaced repetition finds the "sweet spot" that maximizes memory retention for the least amount of effort.

‚öôÔ∏è How To Use It

1

Create Flashcards

Break down material into question-and-answer pairs. "What is...?", "Define...", "Who invented...?"

2

Review & Sort (Leitner System)

Get 3-5 boxes. All cards start in Box 1. If you get a card right, move it to Box 2. If you get it wrong, move it back to Box 1.

3

Space the Reviews

Review Box 1 daily, Box 2 every 2-3 days, Box 3 weekly, and so on. This manually creates the "spaced" intervals.

4

Use Software

Apps like Anki or Quizlet automate this entire process. You simply rate how easy or hard a card was, and the app schedules the next review for you.
