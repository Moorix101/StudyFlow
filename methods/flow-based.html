Flow-Based Note Taking

‚òÖ ‚òÖ ‚òÖ

Flow-Based Note Taking is a free-flowing, non-linear style that captures ideas as they come, using arrows, connections, and minimal structure. This method prioritizes capturing all information quickly during fast-paced lectures or discussions.

üí° What It Is

Flow-based note taking is about capturing information as it flows, without worrying about perfect organization. You write ideas as they come, connect them with arrows, and use minimal structure. The goal is speed and completeness, not neatness.

This method is inspired by the idea that trying to organize notes in real-time can slow you down and cause you to miss information. Instead, you capture everything first, then organize later if needed.

üß† Why It Works

By removing the pressure to organize while taking notes, you can write faster and capture more information. This is especially important during fast-paced lectures where every second counts.

The free-flowing style also allows you to capture connections and relationships as they occur to you, rather than forcing them into a rigid structure. This can lead to more natural and meaningful notes.

üë§ Best For

  • Fast-paced lectures or presentations
  • Brainstorming sessions
  • Discussions or group meetings
  • When you need to capture everything quickly
  • Subjects where connections are more important than structure

‚öôÔ∏è How To Use It

1

Write Continuously

Write down information as it comes, without stopping to organize. Keep your pen moving.

2

Use Arrows and Connections

Connect related ideas with arrows, lines, or simple symbols. Show relationships as you see them.

3

Don't Worry About Neatness

Focus on capturing information, not making it look perfect. Messy is okay if it's complete.

4

Use Abbreviations

Use abbreviations, symbols, and shorthand to write faster. Develop your own system.

5

Organize Later

After the lecture, review your notes and organize them if needed. The initial capture is what matters.
