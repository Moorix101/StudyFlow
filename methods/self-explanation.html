Self-Explanation

‚òÖ ‚òÖ ‚òÖ

Self-Explanation involves verbally explaining how you solve problems or understand concepts as you work through them. This process helps you identify gaps in your understanding and build coherent mental models of how things work.

üí° What It Is

Self-explanation is the process of explaining to yourself (out loud or in writing) how you're solving a problem or understanding a concept as you work through it. You describe your reasoning, the steps you're taking, and why you're making certain decisions.

Unlike simply solving problems, self-explanation requires you to articulate your thought process. This verbalization forces you to make your implicit knowledge explicit, revealing gaps and inconsistencies in your understanding.

üß† Why It Works

When you explain your reasoning, you're engaging in metacognition‚Äîthinking about your thinking. This helps you monitor your understanding and identify when you don't actually know something, even if you can perform the steps.

The process of articulating your thoughts forces you to organize information coherently. You can't explain something you don't understand, so gaps in your explanation reveal gaps in your knowledge.

Research shows that students who self-explain while learning perform better on problem-solving tasks and transfer tests (Chi et al., 1989).

üë§ Best For

  • Problem-solving in math, science, or programming
  • Understanding step-by-step processes
  • Learning how to apply formulas or procedures
  • Building coherent mental models
  • Identifying gaps in understanding

‚öôÔ∏è How To Use It

1

Work Through the Problem

Start solving the problem or working through the concept as you normally would.

2

Explain Each Step Aloud

As you work, explain what you're doing and why. Say things like "I'm doing X because..." or "The next step is Y because..."

3

Identify When You're Stuck

If you can't explain why you're doing something or what comes next, that's a signal you don't fully understand. Stop and figure it out.

4

Connect Steps Together

Explain how each step relates to the previous one and why the sequence makes sense. This builds a coherent mental model.

5

Review Your Explanation

After solving, review your explanation. Could someone else follow it? If not, refine your understanding.

‚úì Pros

  • Reveals gaps in understanding immediately
  • Builds coherent mental models
  • Improves problem-solving transfer
  • Develops metacognitive awareness
  • Works for any procedural knowledge

‚úó Cons

  • Can be time-consuming
  • Requires speaking or writing (not always convenient)
  • May feel awkward explaining to yourself
  • Less effective for simple memorization tasks
  • Requires honest self-assessment
