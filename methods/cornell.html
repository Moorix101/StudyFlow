Cornell Note-Taking

‚òÖ ‚òÖ ‚òÖ

The Cornell Note-Taking System is a highly organized method for taking, organizing, and reviewing notes. It divides your page into three sections: a main note-taking column, a cue column for questions, and a summary section at the bottom.

üí° What It Is

Developed by Prof. Walter Pauk at Cornell University, this system is a complete note-taking and review process in one. The page layout is simple:

‚Ä¢ Note-Taking Column (Right): Where you take your main notes during a lecture or while reading.
‚Ä¢ Cue Column (Left): A narrow column where you write keywords, main ideas, or questions after class.
‚Ä¢ Summary Section (Bottom): A few sentences at the bottom of the page summarizing the entire page's content.

üß† Why It Works

This method forces you to be an active learner. Instead of just passively writing, you must process the information twice: once when taking notes, and again when creating cues and a summary.

The cue column is a built-in study tool. You can cover the main notes section and use the cues to test yourself (a perfect example of Active Recall). The summary section forces you to synthesize the information, which builds deep understanding.

By summarizing, you are engaging in elaboration, connecting new ideas to what you already know, which is a key principle of effective learning.

üë§ Best For

  • Structured, lecture-based classes (like history, science, or economics).
  • Students who want an "all-in-one" system for notes and review.
  • Organizing notes from dense textbook chapters.

‚öôÔ∏è How To Use It

1

Divide Your Page

Draw a horizontal line 2 inches from the bottom. Then, draw a vertical line 2.5 inches from the left edge.

2

Take Notes (Note Column)

During the lecture or reading, take notes in the main (right) column. Use short sentences, abbreviations, and bullet points.

3

Create Cues (Cue Column)

As soon as possible after class, review your notes and write questions, keywords, or main ideas in the narrow (left) column.

4

Summarize (Summary Section)

At the bottom of the page, write a 2-3 sentence summary of the entire page's content in your own words.

5

Review and Recite

To study, cover the right column and use your cues to recite the information aloud. Check your accuracy. This is the most critical step!

üí™ Tips for Success

‚ú®

Be Concise

Use symbols, abbreviations, and bullet points. Don't write full sentences.

‚è±Ô∏è

Write your cues and summary within 24 hours while the information is still fresh.

When studying from your cues, say the answers out loud. It's more effective than thinking them.

  • Making the cue column too wide, which wastes space.
  • Only taking notes but never using the cue/summary sections for review.
