Feynman Technique

‚òÖ ‚òÖ ‚òÖ
Launch Tool

The Feynman Technique is a mental model for understanding complex topics. The core idea is to explain a concept in simple, plain language, as if you were teaching it to someone who knows nothing about it.

üí° What It Is

Named after the Nobel Prize-winning physicist Richard Feynman, this technique is not just a study method but a way of thinking. It's built on the premise that "if you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it well enough."

By forcing yourself to translate complex jargon into simple terms, you expose the gaps in your own knowledge. The process itself builds a deep, intuitive understanding that lasts far longer than simple memorization.

üß† Why It Works

This technique forces you to engage in active recall and elaboration. Instead of passively reading, you must actively retrieve information, connect ideas, and construct a coherent narrative.

When you get stuck, you know *exactly* what you don't understand. This pinpoints your knowledge gaps, allowing you to go back to the source material with a specific question in mind, making your studying far more efficient.

This process of retrieval and reformulation, known as "the generation effect," makes learning more effortful but vastly more effective than passively re-reading.

üë§ Best For

  • Students tackling complex, abstract subjects (like physics or philosophy).
  • Anyone who needs to explain a topic to others (e.g., presentations, teaching).
  • Learners who get "stuck" on jargon and details.

‚öôÔ∏è How To Use It

1

Choose Your Concept

Get a blank sheet of paper and write the name of the concept you want to learn at the top.

2

Teach It to a Child

Write out an explanation of the concept as if you were teaching it to a 12-year-old. Use simple language, short sentences, and analogies.

3

Identify Gaps & Review

When you get stuck or find yourself using complex terms, you've found a gap. Go back to your source material and study until you can explain that part simply.

4

Organize & Simplify

Review your simplified explanation. Read it aloud. If it sounds confusing, refine it. Organize it into a simple story that flows logically.
