80/20 Rule

‚òÖ ‚òÖ ‚òÖ

The 80/20 Rule, also known as the Pareto Principle, suggests that 80% of results come from 20% of efforts. In studying, this means focusing on the 20% of material that will give you 80% of the results. Identify the most important concepts and prioritize deep understanding of these high-impact areas.

üí° What It Is

The 80/20 Rule is a principle that suggests most effects come from a small number of causes. In studying, this means that a small portion of the material (typically around 20%) will be responsible for most of your exam questions or learning outcomes.

By identifying and focusing on this high-impact 20%, you can study more efficiently and achieve better results with less time. This doesn't mean ignoring the other 80%, but rather prioritizing the most important material.

üß† Why It Works

Not all study material is created equal. Some concepts are foundational and appear frequently, while others are less important. By focusing on the high-impact material, you maximize your learning efficiency.

This approach also helps when time is limited. Instead of trying to cover everything superficially, you can achieve deep understanding of the most important concepts, which often leads to better overall performance.

üë§ Best For

  • Limited study time
  • Large amounts of material to cover
  • Exams with clear emphasis on certain topics
  • Efficient learning strategies
  • Prioritizing study efforts

Determine which 20% of topics, concepts, or formulas are most important and appear most often.

3

Spend most of your study time achieving deep understanding of these high-impact areas.

After mastering the 20%, review the remaining 80% more quickly, focusing on key points.

5

Test Your Strategy

Use practice tests to verify that focusing on the 20% gives you strong results.
